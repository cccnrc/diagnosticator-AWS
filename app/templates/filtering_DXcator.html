1. Maximum Allele Frequency
popmax AF: this value specifies the maximum Allele Frequency (AF) that a particular variant can reach in any sub-population. This is screened on gnomAD (exome and genome), ExAC, 1000G, ESP6500, and UK10K: if a given variant AF in any sub-population in any of those databases exceed the selected MAF, then the variant is not considered among possible diagnsotic results. If in doubt and you can use a: 10E-4

2. Maximum Allele Count
max AC: this value specifies the Allele Count (AC) of a variant in your internal population (according with the VCF annotation): if a variant exceeds this number, then it is filtered out from possible diagnostic results

3. Variant Consequences
This parameters allows you to specify which VEP calculated consequences (full list here) you want to exclude from your analysis. As example, you probably don't want to consider in your analysis intergenic or intronic variants when analyzing WGS results, so just select them and they won't show up in results. Be strict in your analysis, you don't wanna spend all your day rewieving negative results.
