    Documentation
    1. Data Privacy
      1. Data Privacy
      Your Diagnosticator local application will only share with the server your username, project name and the variant coordinates (chr-pos-ref-alt) of the accepted variants. In order to allow contact you regarding possible changes about your variants interpretaion (e.g. other users annotating the same variants, changes in publicly available DBs, etc.)
      Every time you accept a variant as disease-causing for a patient you will be asked regarding wether you actually wants to share your variants annotation with the central server or not. Thus, you are always free to choose what you want to share and what you do not want to.
      We strongly encourage you to share your work with the rest of the Diagnosticator community as you will take advantage of other people work as well in your project annotation. Sharing is caring, but always be sure to share what you really want to.
    2. File Storage
      2. File Storage
      All the VCF and genelist files you will use for your clinical annotation on Diagnosticator live locally on the machine you are using to run the Diagnosticator local application.
      This means that you and only you are responsible for any unwanted access to that machine and consequently to all your files stored there (not only Diagnosticator ones). The way the local application is configured expose your application only on your localhost network, meaning that no port will be accessible from outside network on your local machine (unless you modify Diagnosticator configuration files to allow so).
